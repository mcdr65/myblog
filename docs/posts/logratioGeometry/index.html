Sample Space of compositional data

The sample space of compositional data with \(D\) parts is a simplex:

\(\mathcal{S}^D=\{\mathbf{x}=[x_1,x_2,\dots,x_D]\in \mathbb{R}^D \mid x_i>0, i=1,2,\dots,D; \sum_{i=1}^Dx_i=\mathcal{k}\}\)

The only information is given by the ratios between components, so the information of a composition is preserved under multiplication by any positive constant. Therefore, the sample space of compositional data can always be assumed to be a standard simplex, i.e. \(\mathcal{k} = 1\).

Normalization to the standard simplex is called closure and is

\(\mathcal{C}[x_1,x_2,\dots,x_D]=\left[\frac{x_1}{\sum_{i=1}^D x_i},\frac{x_2}{\sum_{i=1}^D x_i}, \dots,\frac{x_D}{\sum_{i=1}^D x_i}\right]\)
